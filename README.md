## Notes on scope and method

Figures are the OBR's March 2026 forecast for 2025-26. Later ONS provisional outturn data (April–May 2026) put borrowing at around £129bn, about £4bn below the £133bn forecast used here; final figures will be revised again.

Defence shows the Ministry of Defence's departmental budget (~£62bn). On NATO's wider definition — which adds items such as armed-forces pensions and some intelligence spending — UK defence spending is around £70bn in 2025 (~2.4% of GDP); that wider measure is the one behind the 2.5%/3.5%-of-GDP commitments.

Groupings. "Other taxes & duties" bundles capital gains, inheritance, stamp, alcohol, tobacco, vehicle, gambling and environmental taxes and more. "Other receipts" is non-tax income. On the spending side, "Other & adjustments" is a large residual covering general public services, most local-authority services, housing, environment, culture and national-accounts adjustments — total managed expenditure exceeds the cleanly-classified functional spend.

The deficit. Borrowing is shown as an inflow so that in = out; the ~£133bn gap between receipts and spending is the structural fact behind most fiscal decisions.


## Sources

OBR Economic and fiscal outlook (Nov 2025) and tax/spending explainers; ONS Public sector finances; House of Commons Library. Verify current figures against the latest OBR/ONS releases before citing.

Assisted by AI for assembling and cross-checking the figures into one consistent structure, then validated by hand against the OBR, ONS and House of Commons Library sources above.
